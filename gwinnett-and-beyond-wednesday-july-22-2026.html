One thing I appreciate about good market information is that it helps replace guesswork with perspective.

Here is one number worth understanding this week, along with a small home-project tip and something local to enjoy.
MARKET SNAPSHOT
Homes are taking a little more time

The median Gwinnett County listing spent 44 days on the market in May 2026, according to Realtor.com. That was about two days longer than one year earlier.

This does not mean homes are not selling. It means buyers have more time to compare options, and sellers may need to be patient and responsive to what the market is telling them.

Good presentation, realistic pricing, and a clear plan for the first few weeks on the market remain especially important.

Pre-send check: Source: Realtor.com Economic Research county inventory data for May 2026, updated June 3, 2026.
HOMEOWNER TIP OF THE WEEK
Measure before the furniture starts moving.

Before buying a large piece of furniture or rearranging a room, write down four quick measurements:

  • The room’s usable wall space
  • Doorway and hallway widths
  • The furniture’s full width and depth
  • Clearance needed for drawers, doors, and walking paths

A few minutes with a tape measure can save a delivery-day surprise — and possibly a scraped doorway.
AROUND GWINNETT: LOCAL EVENT OF THE WEEK

If you are looking for a relaxed local outing, here is one Gwinnett option to check.

Lawrenceville Farmers Market

Downtown Lawrenceville’s seasonal market is a pleasant way to browse local produce and goods while spending a little time around the historic downtown area.

Tip: Check the City of Lawrenceville’s official calendar for the current market date, time, location, parking, and weather updates.
