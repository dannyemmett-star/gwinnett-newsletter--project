MARKET CONDITIONS
More choices, with activity holding steady

Realtor.com reported 3,408 active listings in Gwinnett County during May 2026, up about 11.2% from May 2025. New listings were up about 1.1%, and pending listings were up about 1.8%.

In plain English: buyers have more homes to compare, while the pace of new and pending activity remains close to last year. Sellers should pay close attention to nearby competition, condition, and the first impression their home makes.

The county's median listing price was $459,990, about 1.1% lower than one year earlier. That is an asking-price measure rather than a completed-sale price, but it points to relatively steady pricing while inventory expands.

Source: Realtor.com Economic Research county inventory data for May 2026, released June 3, 2026.
HOMEOWNER ADVICE
Take five minutes before the Fourth.

A simple setup can keep the holiday focused on food, family, and fun:

  • Keep the grill away from siding, deck rails, eaves, and branches.
  • Never leave a lit grill unattended, and keep children and pets clear.
  • Follow local fireworks rules and the directions printed on the package.
  • Keep a garden hose or bucket of water ready, and never relight a firework that did not work.

If you would rather skip the setup entirely, a professional public fireworks show is the easiest step-by-step plan of all.
LOCAL EVENT

Looking for an easy local stop before the holiday celebrations?

Suwanee Farmers Market

The Suwanee Farmers Market is scheduled for Saturday, July 4, from 8:00 AM to noon at Town Center Park. Browse seasonal produce and goods from local farmers and makers, then take a walk around the park.

Check the official City of Suwanee page before leaving for weather, parking, or schedule updates.
