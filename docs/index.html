MARKET SNAPSHOT
More homes are available, while activity remains steady

Realtor.com reported 3,408 active listings in Gwinnett County during May 2026, up about 11.2% from May 2025. New listings were up about 1.1%, and pending listings were up about 1.8%.

That combination suggests buyers have more choices even though the flow of new listings and pending activity remains close to last year's pace. For sellers, nearby competition and a strong first impression matter.

The county's median listing price was $459,990, about 1.1% lower than one year earlier. That is an asking-price measure, not a completed-sale price, but it shows that prices were relatively steady while inventory expanded.

Source: Realtor.com Economic Research county inventory data for May 2026, released June 3, 2026.
HOMEOWNER TIP OF THE WEEK
Set up a safer Fourth of July.

Before the grill is hot or any legal consumer fireworks come out, take a minute to prepare:

  • Keep the grill well away from siding, deck railings, eaves, and overhanging branches.
  • Never leave a lit grill unattended, and keep children and pets away from the cooking area.
  • Follow local fireworks laws and label directions, with a responsible adult supervising.
  • Keep a garden hose or bucket of water ready, and never try to relight a firework that did not work.

A little preparation keeps the celebration focused on family, food, and fun. For a public fireworks show, leave the setup to the professionals.
